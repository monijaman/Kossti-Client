Asset Quality Risk: If the volume of "bad loans" (non-performing loans) increases, the bank will be forced to set aside more money for provisions in the future. This creates pressure on the depositors.

Governance Risk: If there is instability within the Board of Directors or issues with oversight/supervision, the bank's decision-making process becomes weak.

Liquidity Risk: High pressure from bad loans can, at times, create a "cash pressure" (liquidity crunch) for the bank.
